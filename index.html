Digital Storytelling Tools

From Audio to Data Visualization | Medill Washington, Spring 2014

January 13

Elise Hu: Why We’re Here and Social Media Reporting Tools

We’ll lay out the course objectives and do some “best of” show-and-tell, and we’ll spend the second half of the lab experimenting with using free social and blogging platforms for news.

Assignments: 
  • Join Twitter and follow @EliseWho so she can follow you back.
  • Choose a blogging platform to create your blog and start using it.
  • Do some data backgrounding for Matt’s part of the class.
  • Read: The Data Journalism Handbook

January 27

Matt Stiles: Data Journalism and Data Visualization, Part One

What is data journalism? How can you shape data into powerful stories. Understand the difference between good charts and bad charts. Students will get a data-related assignment to include in the final presentations.

Assignments:
  • Don’t forget to keep blogging
  • Acquire crime data from DC.gov
  • Sort, filter and aggregate your data
  • Make a simple interactive chart with Highcharts JS (Send me a link to your interactive chart before next class.)

February 3

Elise Hu: Modern Audio Storytelling and Interactives

The best use of audio storytelling now lives on our tablets and smartphones. Also in this class, we’ll discuss visuals and design, especially since they interplay with audio. Why it matters for journalists and some best design principles to remember. With remaining time in class, check in with Elise on your e-portfolio.

Assignments: 
  • Be prepared for your end-of-class presentation of your blog/e-portfolio on February 24.

February 10

Matt Stiles: Data Journalism and Data Visualization, Part Two

Maps! Everyone loves them, but they’re also getting used irresponsibly. What makes a good map? When should one be used? What should I know about color? An introduction into GIS in journalism and a hands-on workshop with QGIS, Google Fusion Tables and TileMill/MapBox, all of which can power strong maps.

Assignments:
  • Download QGIS;  get free MapBox account
  • Acquire DC neighborhood cluster shapefile, visualize crime rate by neighborhood in static map
  • Publish your map to Fusion Tables or MapBox

February 24

Elise Hu: Back to Fundamentals

In our final meeting, we’ll bring this all home by tying together the reason why understanding these platforms and tools make for the best work today. Students will be asked to make quick presentations showing how they’ve applied one or more of the tools they’ve learned in lab.
